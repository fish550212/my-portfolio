Boost post-silicon validation with no impact on your SoC design flow
Download

  • Improving post-silicon validation quality and effectiveness gives any IC company a competitive advantage. Typical post-silicon validation flows rely on ad-hoc methods and are prone to errors and unpredictable schedules. Woodpecker QED is a systematic and scalable way to improve post-silicon validation productivity and quality without requiring additional expertise or engineering resources. QED Technology Backgrounder
Download

  • Improving post-silicon validation quality and effectiveness gives any IC company a competitive advantage. QED (Quick Error Detection) is a software-driven hardware verification methodology intended to be deployed in pre-silicon verification, post-silicon validation, and manufacturing test to reduce time-to-market, increase quality, and reduce the cost of goods sold (COGS). Using a modified version of the LLVM13 compiler, existing validation test code is enhanced using a number of QED transformations to improve coverage significantly and make bugs dramatically easier to resolve.

Raising the bar on RISC-V sign-off
Download

  • RISC-V has made CPU customization more accessible for SoC designers. It does, however, bring on significant verification challenges. Traditional approaches that rely on random stimulus and simulation are insufficient to meet time-to-market and quality needs. By making the power of formal verification available to anyone, Woodpecker Lockstep helps design and verification engineers detect and resolve more bugs earlier in the process. It has been proven to detect bugs not found by traditional methods, improving verification productivity and the overall sign-off design quality.
